I spent 11 years at Microsoft. That's the origin story. It taught me to think in systems, to respect the weight of production infrastructure, and to understand that the gap between a clever idea and a shipped product is where most technical ambitions quietly die.

What I care about now is harder to name but easier to feel: building at the intersection of cloud, AI, and systems thinking. Not for one cloud — for all three. GCP, AWS, and Azure as peers, each with different strengths, different failure modes, different communities. The multi-cloud practitioner doesn't choose a team. They understand the game.

I work with AI agents as a practical engineering concern, not a theoretical one. The question that interests me is never "can we?" — it's "what breaks when we do, and how do we ship it anyway?"

srvrlss.dev is a fresh start. Not a migration of old posts, not a rebrand of past work — a deliberate blank slate. I'm building in public, thinking in writing, and using this space to work through ideas that matter to practitioners who are tired of hype and hungry for depth.
